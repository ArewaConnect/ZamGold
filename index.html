About Zamfara Gold Market

Founded in 2023, Zamfara Gold Market has quickly established itself as the most trusted platform for natural resources/gold trading in Northern Nigeria. Our mission is to bring transparency, fairness, and professionalism to the natural resources/gold trade while supporting local mining communities.

Our History

Starting as a small buying center in Gusau, we've grown into a full-service natural resources and gold marketplace serving international buyers. Our journey began with a simple goal: to create a fair platform where miners receive proper value for their natural resources and gold.

Our Vision

To become West Africa's leading digital natural resources and gold marketplace by 2030, connecting at least 10,000 artisanal miners directly with global markets while maintaining the highest ethical standards.

Core Values

Integrity: Every transaction is documented and transparent. Community: We reinvest 5% of profits into mining communities. Excellence: Only gold meeting our strict purity standards is traded.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about buying, selling, and trading gold with us.

How do I verify the purity of gold before buying?

+

We provide XRF spectrometer test results for all gold purchases, conducted in your presence if you visit our Gusau office. For remote buyers, we send sealed samples to any accredited lab of your choice at our expense before full payment.

What payment methods do you accept?

+

We accept bank transfers (domestic and international), certified checks, and escrow services for large transactions. Cash payments are limited to ₦5M maximum with proper identification and documentation.

Do you ship gold internationally?

+

Yes, we handle all export documentation and logistics to major markets including Dubai, Turkey, India, and Europe. Minimum export quantity is 1kg for cost efficiency. All exports comply with CBN regulations and include certificates of origin.

How quickly can you pay for gold I want to sell?

+

After purity verification, payment is made within 24 hours for amounts under ₦20M. Larger transactions may take 48-72 hours for compliance checks. We offer instant payment options for trusted partners.

What's your minimum purchase quantity?

+

We buy from 10 grams upwards from individual miners. For refined gold products, our retail division sells from 1 gram for jewelry makers and small investors.

How do you ensure ethical sourcing?

+

We only work with licensed miners and cooperatives. Each transaction includes miner identification, community development levy payments, and excludes conflict zones. Our field officers visit mining sites regularly to verify working conditions.

Legal & Compliance

Our commitment to lawful and ethical operations in the gold trade.

Terms and Conditions

All transactions with Zamfara Gold Market Ltd are governed by Nigerian law and subject to the following terms:

  • Prices are fixed at time of contract signing
  • Payment terms vary by transaction size (50% deposit for orders over ₦10M)
  • Force majeure clauses apply for government actions or natural disasters
  • Disputes resolved through Zamfara State arbitration

Privacy Policy

We collect minimal customer data required for regulatory compliance and transaction processing:

  • Identification documents for transactions over ₦5M
  • Contact information for service delivery
  • Data is never shared with third parties except as required by law
  • Right to access and delete your data upon request

Anti-Money Laundering Policy

As a SCUML-registered entity, we adhere to strict AML/CFT measures:

  • Customer Due Diligence (CDD) for all transactions
  • Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for transactions over $10,000
  • Reporting of suspicious transactions to NFIU
  • Staff training on AML compliance every 6 months

We do not accept cash payments over ₦5M without prior approval and documentation.

KYC Requirements

To trade with us, you'll need to provide:

  • Valid government-issued ID
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Tax Identification Number (for Nigerian entities)
  • Mining license (for sellers)
  • Business registration documents (for companies)
